HARMONIA

A smart support ecosystem for elderly care designed to reduce social isolation.

Project Type Healthcare
Duration 2025
Year 2025
Category UI e UX

Project Overview

The Concept

Harmonia is a smart support ecosystem designed to address the growing challenge of social isolation among elderly individuals in Italy. This project explores the design process behind a digital solution aimed at reducing social isolation and enhancing the quality of life for elderly people living alone.

The aim of this project is to design an empathetic and accessible system that enhances autonomy and emotional wellbeing for elderly people living alone. Through a multi-interface ecosystem, Harmonia improves emotional wellbeing and independence by connecting users with caregivers, community services, and social opportunities.

This project was developed at the College for Creative Studies under the guidance of Professor Frances Sun, with Mattia Fiacco Mazzini as an exchange student.

Problem Statement

The elderly population in Italy faces several significant challenges:

  • Increasing social isolation among the elderly in Italy
  • Difficulties in using modern technology
  • Anxiety related to medical emergencies or daily forgetfulness
  • Caregivers face challenges in remotely monitoring their elderly relatives

By 2030, over 28% of seniors in Italy will live alone, and social isolation is significantly impacting their mental and physical well-being. In Italy, seniors over 65 make up 38% of all suicides, highlighting the severity of the issue.

User Research & Insights

In Europe, millions of elderly people face loneliness every day, profoundly impacting their quality of life and well-being. Our research identified several key challenges:

  • Fear of being forgotten
  • Difficulty understanding complex technology
  • Lack of meaningful daily interactions
  • Technology has the potential to bridge this gap, but current solutions are not accessible to all

Users were interviewed remotely through various contacts in Italy, offering diverse insights into the challenges and needs. The target demographic is elderly individuals over 65 years old who live alone or experience social isolation, often lacking meaningful connections or opportunities to engage with their community.

User Personas

Maria Rossi

Retired and widowed, Maria spends 4-5 days a week completely alone. She prefers simple, repetitive routines and trusts only essential and familiar technology. She has a family that cares for her but is often busy.

Frustrations:

  • Isolation: Spends most days alone, with few social interactions
  • Limited social opportunities: Struggles to find local events that match her interests
  • Technology intimidation: Overwhelmed by complex interfaces
  • Dependence on family: Relies on her children for tech support

Needs:

  • Social Connection: Wants to feel less isolated
  • User-Friendly Tools: Needs intuitive technology
  • Family Support: Caregivers should be able to remotely assist
  • Encouragement to Stay Active: Gentle reminders to maintain an active lifestyle

The Solution

Introducing the Smart Totem

Harmonia is a comprehensive solution that includes:

  • A home device that integrates social interactions, event recommendations, and reminders to keep seniors engaged
  • Seamlessly connected with a mobile app for caregivers
  • A simplified interface for easy access to reminders and social tools for seniors
  • A dashboard for caregivers to manage the device, input reminders, and suggest activities

The Harmonia system connects elderly users, caregivers, and public services through a centralized platform, enabling real-time communication, activity suggestions, and emergency support via home totems, smartwatches, and mobile apps for both family members and social workers.

Design Process

The design process for Harmonia followed a user-centered approach:

  1. Research & Problem Definition:

    Extensive research into the challenges faced by elderly individuals in Italy, including social isolation statistics and technology adoption barriers.

  2. User Personas & Needs Analysis:

    Development of detailed personas like Maria Rossi to understand the specific needs, frustrations, and goals of our target users.

  3. Ideation & Concept Development:

    Exploration of various solutions, leading to the multi-interface ecosystem concept that addresses both elderly users and caregivers.

  4. Low-Fidelity Prototyping:

    Creation of initial sketches and wireframes to visualize the user experience for both the elderly user and caregivers.

  5. High-Fidelity UI Design:

    Development of detailed interface designs with a focus on simplicity, accessibility, and intuitive interaction.
