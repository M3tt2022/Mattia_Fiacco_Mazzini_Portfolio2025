OTIUM

A multifunctional furniture system designed to enhance both relaxation and workspaces.

Project Type Furniture System
Duration 9 months
Year 2022-2023
Category Thesis Project

Project Overview

The Concept

Otium is a multifunctional system designed to enhance both relaxation and workspaces. It features a wireless charging zone, a magnetic spotlight for convenience, and thermoformed felt panels that double as armrests.

A key element of Otium is its adjustable sound-absorbing R-PET panel, allowing users to adapt the space for better acoustic privacy. A mechanism in the backrest maintains ergonomic support via a non-woven fabric membrane when the Shuckra system is activated.

Otium is fully disassemblable and made from recyclable materials, prioritizing sustainability and reducing environmental impact.

The Aim

The aim of the project was to create a versatile product that provides both visual and acoustic privacy, enhancing relaxation and productivity in workspaces.

The product is adaptable for users, allowing breaks from work or soft work activities. By incorporating an adjustable sound-absorbing R-PET panel, the design allows users to customize their environment for improved acoustics, facilitating both focused work and restorative breaks.

The product is fully disassemblable and constructed from recyclable materials, minimizing the environmental impact.

  • Visual and acoustic privacy
  • Adaptable for both relaxation and work
  • Sound-absorbing R-PET panel
  • Wireless charging zone
  • Sustainable materials
  • Fully disassemblable design

External Panels

To conceal the internal structure and integrate the wireless charging function for both a cell phone and the spotlight charging zone, six panels were designed.

The structure is covered both inside and out with PET felt panels, produced through thermoforming. This process allowed for the creation of built-in armrests, enhancing comfort during relaxation mode.

These same thermoformed armrests also enable the soft work configuration, featuring female cavities that securely position the table for work.

The Configurations

01Open Mode

The product is in its open mode: the panel has not been activated, and the external environment is still visible to the user. This setup allows for an easy transition between moments of relaxation and light work, adapting to the user's needs.

02Privacy Mode

The user has chosen to raise up the sound-absorbing panel. The felt element effectively provides both visual and acoustic privacy, crafting the perfect space for the user to take a break from their activities and enjoy a moment of calm.

03Work Mode

A specially designed HPL table can be requested from the office or co-working space staff. By rotating the seat 180°, users can create a quieter, distraction-free environment, thanks to the raised privacy panel, making it a perfect space for focused work.

Technical Details

Otium was designed with a strong focus on sustainability and the end-of-life phase of the product. The chair's structure is made from rotational-molded plastic, with integrated cavities to securely position bio-polyurethane cushions. The entire piece is upholstered with fabric made from recycled PET bottles, sourced from an Italian company.

To accommodate a mechanism in the backrest, two cavities are integrated into the chair's structure, where a non-woven fabric membrane is assembled. This membrane helps secure the back cushion, ensuring it stays in place geometrically when the Shuckra system is activated.

The internal structure of the chair follows Design for Disassembly principles, making it easy to disassemble components at the end of their lifecycle or when repairs are needed. This approach promotes recyclability and extends the product's durability, contributing to a more sustainable solution.
