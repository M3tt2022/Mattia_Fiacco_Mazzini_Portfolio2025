GUARD-ME

A wearable device designed to monitor vital parameters of elderly people during mountain hiking.

Project Type Healthcare
Duration 4 weeks
Year 2023
Category Wearable Technology

Project Overview

The Concept

Guard-me is a wearable device designed to monitor the vital parameters of elderly people during mountain hiking activities. The project was developed during a trainee workshop under the guidance of Professors Suzuki Kazunari and Lorenzo Palegatta.

Working in a team with Alessandro Cesa, Paolo Lunardon, and Martina Ricca, we focused on creating a solution that would allow elderly hikers to continue enjoying outdoor activities while providing peace of mind to both them and their companions.

The device attaches to a backpack and continuously monitors the user's health status, providing real-time feedback through a color-coded system and alerting companions when intervention might be needed.

Research & Context

Our research focused on understanding the needs of elderly walkers in mountain environments, a widely practiced activity both in Western countries and Japan (the main action scenario for our client).

We identified several key user characteristics and needs:

  • Enthusiastic hikers who don't want to accept the limitations of aging
  • Recent cardiovascular issues that create anxiety during physical activity
  • Desire for independence while still having safety measures in place
  • Need for non-intrusive monitoring that doesn't spoil the outdoor experience

The project opportunity emerged: to create an integrable system that allows health status monitoring in real-time without alarming or annoying the user, while providing companions with the information they need to ensure safety.

The Solution

How It Works

Guard-me is a device designed to monitor the vital parameters of elderly people by those accompanying them. The system consists of:

  1. Electronic monitoring patch:

    Applied to the user's body, this patch collects vital data and transmits it to the main device.

  2. Backpack-mounted indicator:

    A circular device that attaches to the user's backpack and displays their health status through an intuitive color-coded system:

    • Green (85%): User is at full strength and can continue the activity
    • Yellow (50%): User is beginning to fatigue and may need a break soon
    • Red (10%): User has reached physical limits and needs to stop
  3. SOS mode:

    In emergency situations, the device emits intermittent sounds and lights to signal the severity of the situation and call for help.

This system allows companions to adjust their pace or advise the elderly user to take breaks based on objective health data rather than subjective assessment.

Materials & Design

Product Details

The Guard-me device was designed with careful consideration of materials and user interaction:

  • Patch for measuring vital levels: A comfortable, skin-friendly sensor that collects health data
  • Rear clip for fixing: Aluminum construction for durability and secure attachment to backpacks
  • Speaker: Integrated audio alert system for emergency situations
  • Color-coded display: Intuitive visual feedback system visible from a distance

The design draws inspiration from various wearable health monitoring devices, combining elements of medical technology with outdoor equipment aesthetics. Materials were selected for durability in outdoor environments while maintaining comfort and ease of use.
