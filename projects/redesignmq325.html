Redesign MQ325

A sustainable redesign of the Braun MQ325 hand blender focused on environmental impact reduction.

Project Type Sustainable Design
Duration 4 weeks
Year 2023
Category Consumer Products

Project Overview

The Concept

This project focused on redesigning the Braun MQ325 Multiquick hand blender with a strong emphasis on environmental sustainability. The goal was to analyze the product's lifecycle and identify opportunities for improvement in terms of material usage, disassembly, and overall environmental impact.

The project was conducted as part of the "Design for Environmental Sustainability" course at Politecnico di Milano, under the guidance of Professors Carlo Proserpio and Valentina Rognoli.

Working in a team with Marialucia De Cagna, Paolo Lunardon, and Martina Ricca, we conducted a Life Cycle Analysis (LCA) of the original product and developed a comprehensive redesign strategy focused on three key areas: materials, assembly, and end-of-life considerations.

The Research

Our research began with a thorough disassembly and analysis of the Braun MQ325 hand blender. This process revealed several challenges in the product's design that impacted its environmental footprint:

  • Difficult disassembly due to numerous components that were welded together and could not be individually recycled
  • Co-stamping issues that made disposal difficult
  • Multiple plastic types that complicated recycling processes
  • Welded components that prevented easy repair or part replacement

Through reverse-engineering and detailed analysis, we identified that the upper unit of the blender was the element with the greatest environmental impact, making it the primary focus for our redesign efforts.

Design Strategy

Our redesign strategy addressed three key problematic areas:

  1. Co-stamping and material issues:

    We replaced fossil elastomers with bio-based alternatives (specifically Terraprene by FKuR) to improve the product's lifecycle. This change not only conserves limited fossil raw materials but also reduces CO2 emissions, with one ton of Green TPE capable of capturing up to 3.09 tons of CO2 from the atmosphere.

  2. Multiple plastic types:

    We standardized the materials of the upper unit, eliminating PP and redesigning all components in ABS. This monomaterial approach offers significant performance advantages: it's easy to print, impact-resistant, and allows for high-quality surface finishes, while also simplifying end-of-life recycling.

  3. Welded components:

    We redesigned the assembly to facilitate disassembly, implementing a two-way snapfit system for motor replacement. This approach enables easier upgradeability and replacement of the engine, extending the product's useful life and reducing waste.

Sustainable Materials

Bio-Based Elastomers

A key innovation in our redesign was the replacement of traditional rubber materials with biobased terraprene. This change involved a complete redesign of the buttons themselves to minimize the amount of material required, aligning with sustainability and environmental impact reduction goals.

The adoption of this biobased thermoplastic elastomer offers significant advantages:

  • Conservation of increasingly limited fossil raw materials
  • Reduction of CO2 emissions
  • Improved end-of-life recyclability
  • Maintained tactile quality and user experience
